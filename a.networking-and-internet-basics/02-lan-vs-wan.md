# LAN vs WAN

## Overview

LAN (Local Area Network) and WAN (Wide Area Network) are two types of computer networks that differ in size, scope, and purpose.

A LAN connects devices within a small, limited area, while a WAN connects networks over large geographical distances.

---

## Why It Matters

Understanding LAN vs WAN is important because:

- It explains how devices are connected locally and globally
- It helps in understanding how the internet is structured
- It is foundational for networking, servers, and infrastructure
- It clarifies how data travels between nearby and distant systems

---

## Key Concepts

- **Network Scope**: The physical or geographical area covered
- **Connectivity**: How devices are linked together
- **Data Transmission**: How data moves within and between networks
- **Infrastructure**: The hardware and systems supporting the network

---

## Local Area Network (LAN)

A LAN is a network that connects devices within a limited area such as a home, office, or building.

### Characteristics

- Small geographic area
- High data transfer speed
- Privately owned and managed
- Low latency (fast communication)

### Examples

- Devices connected within a home network
- Office computers connected to the same network
- School or campus networks

---

## Wide Area Network (WAN)

A WAN is a network that connects multiple LANs over large distances, often across cities, countries, or continents.

### Characteristics

- Large geographic coverage
- Slower compared to LAN due to distance
- Uses public and private infrastructure
- Higher latency compared to LAN

### Examples

- The internet
- Corporate networks connecting multiple offices
- Global communication systems

---

## LAN vs WAN Comparison

| Feature            | LAN                         | WAN                             |
|--------------------|-----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Scope              | Small area                  | Large geographic area            |
| Speed              | High                        | Lower than LAN                   |
| Ownership          | Private                     | Public or shared                 |
| Latency            | Low                         | Higher                           |
| Connectivity       | Direct local connections    | Indirect via multiple networks   |

---

## How They Work Together

- Devices communicate within a LAN for local operations
- LANs connect to other networks through routers
- WANs connect multiple LANs across distances
- The internet is a large WAN connecting millions of LANs

---

## Summary

A LAN connects devices within a small area, providing fast and efficient communication, while a WAN connects multiple networks over large distances.

Together, they form the foundation of modern networking and enable global communication through systems like the internet.
